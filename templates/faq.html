Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Kizuna

Kizuna (çµ†) means "bonds between people" in Japanese. It's a community platform designed for IBDP students to coordinate CAS experiences, discover and join clubs, and organize community events. The platform helps students connect, collaborate, and track their CAS activities in one place.

To register for an event, browse the Events page and find an event you're interested in. Click on the event to view details, then click the "Register" button. You'll receive a confirmation and the event will appear in your profile under "My Registrations." Make sure to check event requirements and deadlines before registering.

CAS stands for Creativity, Activity, Service. It's a core component of the International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme that requires students to engage in experiences across these three strands. CAS encourages students to be involved in activities beyond academics, develop new skills, and contribute to their communities.

  • Creativity: Arts, music, drama, and other creative pursuits
  • Activity: Physical exertion contributing to a healthy lifestyle
  • Service: Voluntary work that benefits others

To create a club, you need to be a registered user. Navigate to the Clubs page and look for the "Create Club" button. You'll need to provide:

  • Club name and description
  • CAS category (Creativity, Activity, or Service)
  • Meeting schedule and location
  • Club logo or image

Your club will be reviewed and approved by administrators before becoming visible to other users.

Kizuna is primarily designed for IBDP students, teachers, and coordinators. Students can use the platform to discover and participate in CAS experiences, join clubs, and track their activities. Teachers and coordinators can create events, manage clubs, and oversee student participation. If you're unsure whether your school is eligible, please contact us at kizuna@cgs.gr.

Your CAS hours are automatically tracked when you register for and attend events through Kizuna. Visit your profile to see a breakdown of your hours across Creativity, Activity, and Service categories. You can also view your event history and export your participation records for your CAS portfolio.
